Parking

Parking brake is a drum brake which is included in the Wilwood Forged Dynapro Low-Profile Rear Parking Brake Kit. The parking brake is actuated via the original parking brake cable and the underdash parking brake lever. It needs attention and proper cable running, because the Lokar brake cables coming from the Wilwoods does not fit well with the original brake cable coming from the front.

Update 2021-01

Front parking brake cable is a factory replica Scott Drake C9ZZ-2853-A.

Rear parking brake cable is a Wilwood 330-9371 kit. The Wilwood kit hooks up directly to the Wilwood e-brake drums in the rear and comes with a cable equalizer and cable block.

The cable block had to be slightly altered in order to be hooked up to the front parking brake cable of the car. The middle thread of the block had to be extended to 8mm inner diameter, so that the original front parking brake cable could be connected to the Wilwood kit.

Carburetor

Carburetor is a Holley Street Avenger 670 in Revision 6. It injects 670cfm via 4 barrels, two primaries, two secondaries. Primaries are mechanical with a 10.5 power valve and 68 main jets. Secondaries are vacuum-initiated using a purple Moroso spring and 72 jets. Carburetor is mounted on a 2” open spacer. Choke is electrical.

Rebuilt 2020-12

Carburetor was rebuilt in December 2020. We swapped in new float needles, because the rubber seals of old ones where brittle and their debris was causing a rough idle. We swapped in new gaskets for both metering blocks and fuel bowls, because the old ones were torn. We swapped in a new primary pump, because diaphragm of the old one was already brittle. We swapped in a new vacuum diaphgram, because the old one was worn out. We swapped in a purple vacuum spring from Moroso, because the old spring was “custom cut” and we experienced a stumble when the secondaries where opening. We increased the primary power valve from 6.5 to 10.5, because we experienced a stumble off the line. We realigned and tested the electric choke. We added an air cleaner gasket, since it was missing.

Clutch

RAM 88994, organic, 1.125” 26-spline input, 12” disc, diaphragm, mechanical actuation. Clutch linkage is mechanical in original Z-Bar style. It’s worn out.

Images

Bell Housing

Bell housing is a C5TA-7505-B iron housing. It is 6 3/4” deep, which is 1/2” deeper than the factory Cobra Jet housing. It has a rectangular opening on the driver side for manual clutch actuation. Due to it’s shape at the opening, we had to slightly modify it to make it work with a 12” clutch. This bell housing has been used mainly for trucks. The aftermarket alternative to this bell housing (in combination with a Ford FE 428 and a TKO 500/600) would be the Quick Time RM-6056SFI.

Images

Transmission

Transmission is a Silver Sports Transmissions Perfect Fit Tremec TKO 600 5-Speed Manual. Gear ratios are 2.87, 1.89, 1.28, 1.00, 0.64. Input is a 26-spline shaft. Shifter is an STX short throw.

Before we installed the SST TKO, the car came with a Ford 4-Speed TopLoader.

Rear axle

Rear axle has been converted to a Moser Engineering 9” MusclePak. It comes with a semi-gloss black heavy-duty housing based on steel tubes with 3” outer diameter and 1/4” walls. Differential is a limited slip Detroit TrueTrac 3.50:1 with 31-splines, which is contained in a gloss red nodular iron case. Mounted brakes are Wilwood Dynapro Low-Pro Rear Parking Brake Kit for 14” and 15” rims.

Bearings are tapered Ford A20 roller bearings. Outer diameter is 3.150” and inner diameter is 1.562”. Typical replacement parts would be Timken SET20, which come with U365L races and U399A bearings.

More details

  • Gear ratio is 3.50:1
  • Wheel bolt pattern is Ford 5 x 4 1/2
  • Stud type and size is 1/2” x 20 x 1 3/4” (.620 Knurl/Disc Brake Stud)
  • Roller Bearings are tapered (SKF SET-20, 9BR20, SET20, A20)
  • Race is an U365L, bearing is a U399A
  • Pinion yoke is 1350
  • Fill bung and drain bung are present
  • Brake rotors are drilled, slotted, and Zinc coated. (Why? Because we can.)

History

Before we swapped the Moser in, the car came with an yet-to-be-identified Ford 8.8” rear end.
